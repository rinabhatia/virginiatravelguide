Virginia: A Travel Guide

History and Background

Virginia was founded in 1788 and is home to the Jamestown, the first English settlement in North America. It was one of the original 13 colonies. Its capitol is Richmond. The state is well known for its many historical landmarks and is a tourist attraction due to to its beautiful parks and scenery. The cardinal is the state bird.

Top 10 Things To Do in Virginia "

1. Shenandoah National Park

This park extends along the Blue Ridge Mountains and contains several hiking trails. The trails are surrounded by forests and contains several waterfalls and mountain peaks. Shenandoah is a home to several animals such as birds, deer, and black bears. The park is often blanketed in snow during the winter.

2. Mount Vernon

This was the plantation that George and Martha Washington owned. The beautiful estate is on the banks of the Potomac River in Fairfax County. The architectural includes Neoclassical, Georgian, Palladian styles.

3. Luray Caverns

This site is a cave west of Luray, Virginia. It was discovered in 1878. The underground cavern contains peleothems such as columns, mud flows, stalactites, stalagmites, flowstone, and mirrored pools.It was designated as a world history site in 1973.

4. Monticello

This was a plantation and mansion owned by the 3rd president of the United States, Thomas Jefferson. He began building the Monticello at the age of 26 since he had inherited the land from his father. Today the site holds many tours and is used as a sort of musuem.

5. Burke Lake Park

This lake is located in the city of Burke and includes several picnic areas, lakes, and playgrounds. It is a well designed park located in Fairfax County. The park holds a winter wonderland event during the month of December.

6. Great Falls Park

This is a small national park that extends for 800 acres. It is near the banks of the Potomac in northern Fairfax Country. It is a part of the George Washington Memorial Parkway.

7. Lake Accotink Park

This is lake is located in Fairfax County. The park offers bike rentals, a golf course, picnic areas, and several trails. They offer several holiday themed events.

8. Maymont

This is a 100 acre Victorian estate and park located in Richmond. It is home to the Maymont Mansion. It is now considered a historic house musuem, garden, and a carriage collection. It contains wildlife exhibits and a nature center. The offer carriage rides, tours, and they hold Christmas events. There is a Maymont Mansion Holiday Tour which is very festive.

9. Norfolk Botanical Garden

This is a garden located in Norfolk, Virginia. There are 7 miles of paved paths to explore. It is surrounded by water bodies. They offer tram tours and boat tours. During the holiday season, the have an event called Gardens of Light.

10. Olde Town Carriages

Here, old fashioned horse drawn carriages carry passengers down the two offered tours, the Caroline tour and the Washington Avenue tour. The rides last about an hour each. Grinch Rides are also offered, in which you ride with the Grinch on a horse drawn carriage for 15 minutes down Caroline Street. This site is located in Fredericksburg, Virginia.

Top 5 Restaurants

1. Pink Cadillac Diner

This diner is located in Natural Bridge, Virginia, and is well known for its comfort food and old fashioned snacks. The diner is inspired by the 50s and is very vintage. It is known for its specials such as The Elvis Burger and its several Cadillac Platters.

2. The Virginia Diner

This diner has been open for 85 years, and is located in Wakefield, Virginia. They offer freshly baked breads and deserts as well as comfort food like soup and sandwiches. Their menu offers a variety of meal options.

3. Pop’s Diner

This restaurant is in Virginia Beach and is well known for their breakfast options. The offer breakfast, lunch, and dinner.

4. Bob & Edith’s Diner

This diner is located in Springfield, Virginia. Its decor centers around the 50s time period. They are open all day and serve many old fashioned meals.

5. Pocahontas Pancakes & Waffle House

This diner is known as the best breakfast place in Virginia Beach. It opened in 1974 and is known for its pancakes and waffles. The diner’s walls are painted with murals and paintings.
